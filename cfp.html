Scope and Focus

We welcome submissions showcasing unpublished, original work in areas related to Intelligent Computing, Control Systems, Communication Networks, and Secure Engineering. As the digital landscape evolves, the convergence of these fields becomes increasingly critical. ICICCC-2026 aims to explore these intersections, encouraging papers that address challenges in Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, 5G/6G Networks, IoT, Blockchain, and Cyber-Physical Systems. Whether your research focuses on theoretical frameworks or practical industrial applications, this conference offers a global stage to share your insights and contribute to the next generation of digital ecosystems.

Track Details

This track focuses on the latest advancements in communication systems, including wireless, mobile, optical, and satellite communications. It covers emerging network architectures, 5G/6G technologies, and software-defined networking (SDN) to facilitate seamless data transmission. Research in network security and next-generation internet protocols is also highly encouraged.

This track explores the development of intelligent systems, machine learning algorithms, and expert system frameworks. It highlights innovations in deep learning, neural networks, and automation for smart engineering applications. Contributions should focus on leveraging AI to drive intelligent decision-making and autonomous solutions.

Addressing critical challenges in digital infrastructure, this track focuses on cybersecurity, cryptography, and resilient system design. Topics of interest include blockchain technology, privacy preservation, and secure hardware/software architectures. The track aims to showcase methods for protecting data and mitigating emerging cyber threats.

This track highlights the convergence of Big Data processing and IoT ecosystems. Key focus areas include cloud and edge intelligence, smart sensing, and IoT-enabled applications for smart cities and industries. Research exploring predictive analytics and real-time data processing in connected environments is welcome.
